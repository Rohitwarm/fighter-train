01 — Training

THE
EXERCISES

!

Core Fighter Rule

Use controlled intensity — not "destroy myself daily". Train 3 rounds with 60–90 sec rest between. This becomes conditioning on its own.

Push & upper body
01
Pushups
15–20 reps · 3 rounds
Full range of motion. Tight core throughout. Controlled descent — no flopping. Later progress to explosive pushups.
Upgrade → Explosive pushups
02
Bicep Curl
12–15 reps · 3 rounds
Biceps are secondary in combat. No need to go to failure daily — controlled reps protect your recovery capacity for boxing.
03
Tricep Overhead Extension
12–15 reps · 3 rounds
Critical for boxers — punch extension is almost entirely tricep-driven. The jab, the cross, the hook all depend on this muscle.
ADD
Pullups or Dumbbell Rows
8–12 reps · 3 rounds
Currently missing from your routine. Without pull movements, too much pushing creates rounded shoulders and imbalance that hurts your guard.
⚠ Critical Gap — Add Now
Shoulders — rotate A/B

Doing all three shoulder planes daily causes overuse. Rotating A/B gives each angle recovery time while keeping frequency high enough for boxing guard strength.

Core & conditioning
04
Plank
Build to 90 seconds
Progress gradually: 40 sec → 1 min → 90 sec. Anti-rotation stability from planks directly transfers to a stable boxing base.
05
Sit-ups → Upgrade
20–30 reps
Good start. Later upgrade to leg raises, Russian twists, and bicycle crunches — they train the rotational core that powers hooks and uppercuts.
Upgrade → Russian twists
06
Mountain Climbers
30–40 reps (→ timed 30 sec)
Excellent fighter conditioning exercise. Combines core stability with cardio demand. Later move to timed rounds for metabolic conditioning.
07
Squats
20–25 reps · 3 rounds
Fighter legs need both strength and explosive capacity. Later add jump squats and lunges to develop the power needed for offensive bursts.
Upgrade → Jump squats + Lunges
Add these over time
+1
Jump Rope
5–10 min daily
The single best daily add-on. Massively improves footwork rhythm, timing, and fight-pace stamina. Start week 2.
+2
Running
2–3 times per week
Best exercise for fight stamina. Builds the aerobic base that keeps you calm in round 3. Start with 20 min easy pace, build from there.
+3
Sprint Intervals
Add at month 2+
Explosive cardio that simulates fight bursts. Add only after you've built the aerobic base through running. 8 × 20 sec all-out sprints.

Basic Combinations — Phase 1

Month 1
01
Foundations — Form Before Force
3–5 rounds of shadowboxing at 2–3 min each. Focus on movement, guard position, jab, and cross. Speed comes later — form first. Add 5–10 min of footwork drills: forward, backward, angles, pivot. Practice slips, rolls, and guard recovery daily.
Shadowboxing Guard Jab + Cross Footwork Slips
Month 2–3
02
Combinations & Defence
Begin 1–2–3 combinations (jab-cross-hook). Work on head movement in combinations. Introduce the uppercut. Start developing counter-punching: slip then jab, roll then cross. Bag work begins if available.
1-2-3 Combos Uppercut Head movement Counter-punching Bag work
Month 4–6
03
Speed, Power & Sparring Readiness
Full combination chains. Timed rounds at fight pace (3 min on, 1 min off). Speed development through pad work if accessible. Sparring preparation: controlled light sparring with focus on defence and range control.
Speed work Timed rounds Pad work Light sparring Range control

04 — Nutrition

FIGHTER
DIET

Your goal
RECOMPOSITION
Not bulking. Not cutting. All three simultaneously.
↓
Fat loss
↑
Muscle gain
↑
Stamina
PROTEIN
Daily protein — non-negotiable
Protein is what rebuilds muscle between sessions. Without it, you're training hard and repairing slowly.
  • Eggs (whole) — cheap, complete amino profile
  • Chicken / lean beef — high density sources
  • Greek yoghurt — convenient, good post-session
  • Lentils / chickpeas — plant-based option
  • Fish (tuna, salmon) — protein + omega-3
CARBS
Fuel your training — quality carbs
Carbs power your boxing sessions. Don't cut them — just choose quality sources timed around training.
  • Rice — staple energy source, easy to portion
  • Oats — slow-release, great pre-training
  • Sweet potato — nutrient-dense fuel
  • Fruit (banana, apple) — fast energy + micronutrients
  • Whole wheat bread — practical, convenient
HYDRATION
Water — more than you think
Boxing dehydrates heavily. Even mild dehydration kills speed, reaction time, and punch power.
  • Drink 500ml before training
  • Sip continuously during sessions
  • Rehydrate fully within 1 hour post-session
  • Coconut water for electrolytes on hard days
TIMING
When to eat
Nutrient timing matters for recomposition. Getting this right amplifies everything else.
  • Pre-session: carbs + small protein 60–90 min before
  • Post-session: protein + fast carbs within 40 min
  • Before bed: slow protein (cottage cheese, eggs)
  • Don't train fasted — it limits power output
05 — Mistakes

WHAT TO
AVOID

Training to failure daily
Causes nervous system fatigue, slower punches, shoulder exhaustion, and poor recovery. Use controlled intensity — stop 2 reps before failure.
All shoulders every day
Front raise + side raise + rear delt all together daily overfatigues the shoulder joint. Boxing requires shoulder endurance — protect it with A/B rotation.
Skipping pull movements
An all-push routine creates rounded shoulders and imbalance. This is the biggest structural flaw in your current routine — fix it immediately.
Bulking phase
Extra bodyweight slows footwork and hurts your cardio. You should recompose, not bulk. Lean and fast beats heavy and strong for combat sports.
Chasing maximum muscle
Hypertrophy-focused training conflicts with boxing performance. Muscle that doesn't translate to speed and endurance is dead weight in a fight.
Skipping rest day
Adaptation happens during rest, not during training. Skipping Sunday doesn't make you tougher — it just makes you slower and more prone to injury.
Speed before technique
Fast, wrong punching builds bad habits that are very hard to unlearn. Slow, correct, deliberate — let speed come naturally as form becomes automatic.
Dehydration during training
Even 2% dehydration reduces punch speed, reaction time, and power output. Drink before you're thirsty — thirst means you're already behind.
Neglecting legs
Power starts from the ground. Weak legs = weak punches, slow movement, poor balance. Fighters who ignore leg training plateau quickly.
06 — Wisdom

TIPS &
TRICKS

01
Record yourself shadowboxing
Your guard drops, your chin rises, your hips don't rotate — you can't feel these things, but a phone camera shows them instantly. Record weekly and compare.
02
Round format as conditioning
Stop thinking of rest as weakness. The 60–90 sec rest between rounds is the training stimulus — it forces your body to recover quickly, which is exactly what fighting demands.
03
The jab is your most important punch
It sets distance, creates openings, disrupts rhythm, and accumulates damage. Every world-class fighter has an excellent jab. Spend 40% of your boxing practice on this one punch.
04
Sleep is the most anabolic thing you can do
7–8 hours is not optional. During sleep: testosterone peaks, growth hormone pulses, muscle tissue repairs, and motor skills from training consolidate. Cutting sleep cuts results.
05
Footwork first, power second
Every great punch starts with correct positioning. If you're out of range or off-angle, no power in the world helps. Spend as much time on footwork as on punching — you'll be glad you did.
06
Track your rounds, not just your reps
Progress in boxing is measured in rounds completed at high quality, not reps on paper. If you got through 5 clean shadowboxing rounds today vs 3 last week, you improved — celebrate that.
07
Breathe out on every punch
Exhaling sharply on every strike tightens your core, protects against body shots, and prevents gassing out. It feels mechanical at first — after a month it becomes automatic.
08
Relax between exchanges
Tense fighters gas out in round 2. Tension burns energy; relaxation conserves it. Shake your hands between combinations, drop your shoulders, breathe. Explosiveness comes from sudden tension, not sustained tension.
07 — The Journey

6 MONTH
ROADMAP

Month 1
Foundation & Form
Build the habits. Master the stance, guard, and the jab. Your body is adapting — don't push too hard yet. Focus on technique, not intensity. Add the missing pull exercise immediately.
Orthodox stance locked in Consistent jab mechanics 5 rounds of shadowboxing Add pullups/rows Jump rope 5 min daily
Month 2
Combinations & Cardio
Introduce the 1-2 combination with fluency. Begin running 2–3x per week. Your stamina should noticeably improve. Increase plank to 60 seconds. Upgrade sit-ups to Russian twists.
1-2 combo fluid Running 3x/week Plank 60 seconds Visible upper body changes
Month 3
Power & Defence
Introduce the hook. Work on slipping and rolling as second nature. Add sprint intervals to conditioning. Your physique is recomposing — leaner face, better posture, visible shoulder definition.
1-2-3 combinations Sprint intervals added Slips and rolls natural Leaner physique visible
Month 4
Speed Development
Same technique, faster execution. You've built the neural pathways — now sharpen them. Jump squats replace regular squats. Bicycle crunches replace sit-ups fully. Explosive pushups added.
Noticeably faster punches Jump squats mastered Explosive pushups Footwork automatic
Month 5
Fight Conditioning
Full 3-minute rounds at close to fight pace. Your boxing has become a real skill, not just exercise. Upper abs visible. Stamina to sustain boxing rounds without significant fatigue. Uppercut introduced.
5 rounds at fight pace Uppercut in combinations Upper abs visible High fight stamina
Month 6
Athletic Fighter
The goal. You are now a legitimately dangerous athletic fighter type. Lean, fast, strong, technical. Sparring readiness if desired. The physique and capability you wanted when you started are now real.
Lean athletic physique Real boxing skill High explosive stamina Sparring-ready Visible abs + shoulders Elite confidence
